About

Even growing up, I considered myself a creative type. I made up songs and wrote stories. In college, I took photography classes and found a passion for psychology and social sciences. I was enthralled with human behavior, neurological processes and our relationship to the world around us.

Over the next decade, I honed my customer service skills - actively listening, discovering the root problem and increasing empathy. I wanted to weave more creativity into my work, so I took up front end development at Skillcrush, and then a visual UX design apprenticeship at Bloc.

Being in the field of User Experience, I'm at the intersection of my skills and interests, at a time when interacting with the end user is more important than ever. I enjoy working through a process, gathering data, and turning it into a product or experience that improves people's lives.
