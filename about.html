About Me

Growing up, I considered myself a creative type. I sang, made up dance routines with my sister, and wrote stories. In college, I took photography classes and discovered a passion for psychology and social sciences. I was enthralled with human behavior, neurological processes, and our relationship to the world around us.

Over the next decade, I honed my customer service skills - practiced active listening, learned to uncover the root of the problem, developed greater patience and increased empathy.

I also traveled and relocated to larger cities which broadened my worldview and grew my cultural competency.

I'm the first to admit I had a hard time finding my place, partly because I wanted to do everything from becoming a chef to working on cars. So in 2014, when Obama said everyone needs to learn to code, I thought why not me? I built a Geocities website back in the day!

I tested the waters first, dipping my toe into Codecademy. Then I entered a Web Development program at Skillcrush followed by a fully immersive apprenticeship at Bloc.

At Bloc, I worked with a Senior Designer twice a week. Under her mentorship, I executed user-focused projects considering all elements of the design process from ideation to iteration, making changes based on user feedback and testing.

As it turns out, working with customers to address their needs and solve their problems was the perfect training ground for my design career. My desire to learn all the things? It's helped me pick up different coding languages and software programs and fuels my continuing educational pursuits.

I'm so excited to be in the field of User Experience, at the intersection of my skills and interests, at a time when interacting with the end user is more important than ever. I enjoy working through a process, gathering data, and turning it into a product that makes people's lives easier.
